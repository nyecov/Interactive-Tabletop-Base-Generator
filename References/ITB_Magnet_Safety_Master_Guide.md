## 3. Surface Selection: GSW Mats
Your choice of storage surface dictates the "Safety Factor" of your collection.

*   **GSW Steel Rubber Sheet**: **RECOMMENDED**. Acts as a ferrous surface. Provides high friction and high magnetic "snap." Neodymium magnets perform at ~80% of their theoretical max.
*   **GSW Magnetic Sheet**: **NOT RECOMMENDED** for models over 100g. It uses a "Magnet-on-Magnet" interaction which is significantly weaker (~25% of steel).

## 5. Physical Fitment & Capacity Warnings âš ï¸
A "Fitment Check" has been performed on the matrix. While standard counts are safe, certain high-density configurations will cause OpenSCAD to fail or the base to break.

### ðŸš© The "Impossible" (IP) Configurations
Configurations marked **IP** in the tables failed the geometric capacity test.
-   **Narrow Oval Constraint**: On the **75x42mm Oval**, using magnets larger than 8mm in a multi-magnet configuration (3+) is impossible. The "Center-to-Ring" gap drops below the required 0.8mm safety wall.
-   **20x3mm Radius Overflow**: On smaller ovals, the 20mm magnet's own radius exceeds the base width once the 2mm outer wall is factored in.

### ðŸ’¡ Capacity Tips
-   **If you need more pull on a small base**: Do not add more magnets. Instead, switch to a **smaller number of stronger magnets** (e.g., use 1x 12mm instead of 4x 8mm).
-   **Wall Thickness**: If you override the `min_outer_wall_mm` to less than 1.5mm, you may fit more magnets, but the base will be prone to cracking during magnet insertion.

## 6. Vertical Fitment (Height) Constraints ðŸ“
Larger magnets (10x3mm, 12x3mm) require more vertical space than standard infantry bases (4mm thickness).

| Magnet Thickness | Min. Required Base Height | Logic (Magnet + Recess + Top Shell) |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **2.0 mm** | **4.0 mm** | 2.0 (Mag) + 0.7 (Recess) + 1.3 (Remaining) |
| **3.0 mm** | **4.5 mm** | 3.0 (Mag) + 0.7 (Recess) + 0.8 (Safety Cap) |
| **4.0 mm** | **5.5 mm** | 4.0 (Mag) + 0.7 (Recess) + 0.8 (Safety Cap) |

> [!NOTE]
> The ITB generator includes **Auto-Correction Architecture**. If you set a `base_height_mm` that is too thin for your chosen magnet thickness, the script will automatically increase the height to the minimum viable level to protect the top shell.
